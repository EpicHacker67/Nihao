Who We Are

Our Mission: Give Where You Live

NiHao Food Bank Initiative is a volunteer-driven grassroots movement, focused on raising awareness about hunger in the USA, improving community engagement, as well as directing resources and contributions toward North Texas Food Bank (NTFB) to fight hunger.

Our initiative galvanizes and mobilizes resources of the Chinese diaspora by:

  • Increasing hunger awareness
  • Organizing volunteers
  • Conducting food drives
  • Raising crucial funds

Led by a steering committee comprised of passionate supporters and volunteers, the NiHao movement operates in a non-political, non-religious, robust ecosystem that puts the cause ahead of individual egos.

Our Story

Mobilizing a Force for Good

In the holiday season of 2020, national news outlets reported a staggering headline: "Thousands in Texas line up in cars to receive food before Thanksgiving 2020". On one of the biggest holidays in the US symbolizing abundance and warmth, cars lined up for miles in one of the biggest food insecurity crises in modern history.

COVID-19 caused the highest unemployment rates seen in decades, adding to the food insecurity strain in North Texas. Local Chinese visionaries and friends knew something had to be done.

Our movement was born from a commitment to the deeply held Chinese cultural values of kindness, wisdom, and mutual aid to uplift our community. The DFW Metroplex, with the largest quantitative increase in the Asian American population in the US, holds a powerful and growing force for social good.

Determined to help with hunger relief caused by the pandemic, NiHao Food Bank Initiative became more than a dream in the early spring of 2021.

Our Growth

Building Momentum

As the years moved on, our movement began to gain traction, supported by like-minded philanthropists and local Chinese American families.

Since our launch in 2021, more than 5,000 Chinese American families in the DFW area have joined the fight against hunger.

Together, NiHao and the Chinese American community have provided over 2.6 million meals for the North Texas community over four years.

NiHao stays committed to our central goal over the years: mobilizing your force to give where you live.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is NiHao Food Bank Initiative?

NiHao is a volunteer-driven grassroots movement focused on raising awareness about hunger and directing resources to the North Texas Food Bank. We mobilize the Chinese American community and beyond to fight hunger in North Texas.

How does NiHao work with the North Texas Food Bank?

NiHao is a cultural affinity program of NTFB. All financial contributions made through NiHao go directly to the North Texas Food Bank, and donors receive tax receipts directly from NTFB.

How can I get involved?

There are many ways to participate! You can volunteer at our monthly packing days, become a Youth Ambassador, participate in our fundraising campaigns, or make a direct donation. Visit our Join & Pledge page to learn more.

Are donations tax-deductible?

Yes! All financial contributions made through NiHao are processed directly by the North Texas Food Bank. Donors receive a tax receipt and acknowledgment directly from NTFB, and all donations are tax-deductible to the extent allowed by law.
