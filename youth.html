About the Program

What is the Youth Ambassador Program?

Launched in 2021, the NiHao Youth Ambassador Program is one of the important pillars for the NiHao Food Bank Initiative. It is designed for the young generation to promote awareness of hunger issues, support the NiHao Food Bank program, and provide opportunities for full-time students to develop leadership skills.

As of 2025, the program includes approximately 200 youth ambassadors who contribute significant volunteer hours and fundraising impact.

FAQ

Youth Ambassador FAQ

Who can become a Youth Ambassador?

The program is open to students in Grades 6-12, as well as adults and families who want to participate alongside youth members.

What is the time commitment?

Ambassadors are encouraged to participate in monthly packing days and key fundraising campaigns. The level of involvement is flexible based on your schedule.

Is there a fee to join?

Yes, to ensure every Ambassador starts with a tangible commitment, students are required to make a $300 initial pledge directly to the North Texas Food Bank.

How do I earn volunteer hours?

Volunteer hours are tracked through participation in packing days, fundraising activities, and community events. Hours are certified by the North Texas Food Bank.

What leadership opportunities are available?

Active ambassadors can apply for Youth Leadership Team positions, lead fundraising campaigns, and mentor newer ambassadors.
